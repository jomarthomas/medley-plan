# Training Plan for the BAA Medley Races (2026)

This plan combines running workouts and compound strength training to prepare for the BAA Medley race series: 5K, 10K, and Half Marathon.

## Overview

The goal is to build:
- endurance for the half marathon
- speed for the 5K and 10K
- lower-body strength and stability
- consistency without overcomplicating the schedule

## Warm-Up Routine

Use this before runs or strength sessions.

| Exercise | Duration / Reps |
|----------|----------------|
| Light jog or brisk walk | 5 minutes |
| Leg swings | 10 each leg |
| Bodyweight squats | 10 reps |
| Walking lunges | 10 steps |
| Hip circles | 10 each direction |

**Total warm-up time:** 5–8 minutes

## Long Run Progression

| Phase | Long Run Distance |
|------|-------------------|
| Early training | 8–10 km |
| Mid training | 10–14 km |
| Pre-race phase | 14–18 km |

## Speed Workout Progression

| Phase | Workout |
|------|---------|
| Early training | 6 × 400 m |
| Mid training | 8 × 400 m |
| Later phase | 5 × 800 m |
| Pre-race phase | 3 × 1 km |

## Weekly Training Summary

| Category | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| Running sessions | 3 |
| Strength sessions | 3 |
| Rest days | 1 |
| Weekly running distance | ~12–40 km |
| Strength focus | Compound lifts + unilateral stability |

## Notes

- Keep easy runs easy.
- Increase long-run volume gradually.
- Focus on good form during strength sessions.
- Use Saturday as a true recovery day when needed.
- Adjust volume if fatigue builds too quickly.
